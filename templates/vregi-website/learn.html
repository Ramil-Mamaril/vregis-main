Vocal Fry

Vocal fry is the lowest register (tone) of your voice characterized by its deep, creaky, breathy sound.

Chest Voice

The term “chest voice” refers to the register in which most people speak and sing low to medium-high pitches.

Head Voice

A register above chest voice, refers to the sort of singing voice that gives a ringing feeling in the singer's head.

Falsetto

Falsetto is a mode of singing that sounds breathy, flutey and hollow. It’s usually found in the upper registers of male and female singers.

Mixed Voice

Mixed voice is when you combine your head voice and chest voice to create an even singing tone from the bottom to the top of your voice.

Whistle Register is the highest vocal register created by the isolated vibration of the anterior section of the vocal cords.
